Fò Gineer ch’è Scià Febreer

Festival identity project – independent work, Moggio (LC), Italy, 2025
Disciplines: poster design and architectural scale model

Fò Gineer ch’è Scià Febreer is a winter festival held in Moggio, a small mountain village in the Valsassina valley in northern Italy. The ritual takes place outdoors at night during the coldest period of the year and uses fire, noise and collective presence to mark a transition from winter toward renewal. The goal of this project was to translate the atmosphere and spatial qualities of the ritual into two elements: a poster and a temporary entrance structure conceived at scale. Rather than designing a permanent object, the focus was on creating a spatial condition that could coexist with the landscape and disappear once the ritual ends.

Fire, darkness and human scale were treated as primary design elements. The poster explores this contrast: darkness as a dominant condition interrupted by concentrated points of light. The imagery abstracts the presence of fire and gathering into blurred forms, allowing ambiguity and depth to guide interpretation. The entrance acts as a threshold between the surrounding darkness and the ritual space. From the outside the structure appears compact and opaque, gradually opening toward the interior where fire and gathering take place. Rather than functioning as a gateway, it becomes a slow spatial transition preparing the body to move from exposure into collective warmth.
