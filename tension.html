Tension

Object design project – independent work, Italy, 2025
Materials: steel, marble, tension cables, translucent panels

This project began with a single photograph. I identified proportions, structural logic and material behaviour together with more intangible qualities like tension, balance and fragility. These elements were abstracted and reinterpreted into a chair composed of straight, modular elements connected by articulated joints. Steel cables replace the spider’s web, becoming a primary structural component rather than a secondary support. A relatively heavy seat is held in place by tension, echoing the contrast between the spider’s weight and the apparent lightness of its web. Tension is not concealed but made visible, becoming both a structural and conceptual element of the object.
