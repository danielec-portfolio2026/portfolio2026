Approach

I approach design as a process of translation rather than invention. My work begins with observation—often through photography—where I identify spatial, material and emotional qualities within a context. These qualities are extracted, abstracted and detached from their original form, becoming a set of tensions, proportions and relationships. From this distilled framework I reconstruct them into new mediums, objects, garments or visual systems. I am interested in coexistence: light and dense, fragile and structural, public and intimate. Rather than emphasising spectacle, my practice focuses on restraint, process and the quiet logic that allows different elements to shape one another over time.
